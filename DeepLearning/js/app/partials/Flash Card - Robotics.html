What is an Acuator?

Answer - Acuator

It is a type of motor that
is responsible for moving or
controlling a mechanism or system

Proprioceptive sensors

Answer - Proprioceptive sensors

Measure value internally to system(robot)
Examples: motor speed, wheel load, battery status and stall

Exteroceptive sensors

Answer - Exteroceptive sensor

Information from the robot's environment.
Example: distances to objects and intensity of ambient light

Internal Scribbler Sensors

Answer - Internal Scribbler Sensors

getBattery(), getStall(), getName(), setName()

IR Obstacle Sensors

Answer - IR Obstacle Sensors

Scribbler has two IR Obstacle sensors under
the light sensors that return a binary value
0 or 1. Scribbler has 1 IR receiver located
in the center, and two emitters located on
left and right side of the emitter.
